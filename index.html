The Mileage Difference Between City and Highway Driving

Despite continuing advancements in hybrid and electric-car technology, the good old-fashioned internal-combustion engine is still great. In fact, affordable, fuel-efficient, gasoline-powered cars still outnumber electric cars. The fuel economy of a vehicle takes into account the miles traveled by car and the amount of gasoline consumed while doing traveling. Generally, you get better gas mileage on highways rather than on city streets. For those who want to spend less on gasoline without forking over big money on a hybrid or electric vehicle, this page shows the comprehensive statistics of fuel consumption by different engine cylinders of car models.
